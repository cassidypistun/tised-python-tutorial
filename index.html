Getting started

How to open the notebooks

Click "Launch Notebooks" above

The button opens Binder — a free service that builds a live Jupyter environment directly in your browser.

Wait 1–2 minutes for it to start

Binder builds your environment from scratch. You'll see a loading screen — this is normal. Open it before class so it's ready when you need it.

Open a notebook from the file panel

You'll land in Jupyter Lab. Double-click Notebook_1_The_Interface.ipynb to begin. Work through the notebooks in order.

Run cells with Shift + Enter

Click inside any grey cell and press Shift + Enter to run it. The output appears directly below. That's all you need to get started.

Note on saving your work: Binder sessions are temporary — if you close the tab, your changes are lost. That's fine for this orientation. In your courses, you'll work locally in Anaconda on your own computer where everything saves normally.
